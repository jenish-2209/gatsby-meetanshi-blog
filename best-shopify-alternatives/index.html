Best Shopify Alternatives For Your E-Commerce Store

Published on: 29/8/2024

Author: Shivbhadrasinh Gohil

Shopify is more than enough for many stores at the start, you’ll see that in due course of time, you start getting limited by Shopify.

The alternatives to Shopify, which we will be discussing in this blog, are more advanced and don’t have restrictions with compelled transaction fees.

So, let’s discuss in-depth.

Why Do You Need to Switch From Shopify?

While Shopify has been considered user-friendly and feature-rich, it has its share of drawbacks. A proper understanding of these limitations will help you determine whether or not a different platform is going to serve you better.

Here is a more detailed look at some of the main reasons businesses look to move away from Shopify:

Excessive Transaction Fees

One of the major drawbacks of the platform’s offerings, Shopify transaction fees, depending on your arrangement, range between 0.5% and 2% of every sale made, save for using Shopify Payments. Most Shopify alternatives, on the other hand, have little to no transaction costs, which would be rather significant, particularly for high-volume sellers.

Limited Customization 

Shopify provides a range of themes and apps, but the customisation options can be limiting. While themes are editable and applications can be used to extend more functionalities, many times, this isn’t the answer for everything you want to customize for a better customer experience.

Expensive Scalability 

There could be potential for finding that the costs of working with Shopify are quite ballooning when a business grows. The advanced features in higher-level plans are available at an extra price for a growing business, which can be pretty expensive in the long run. Some alternatives are able to provide more cost-effective scalability through advanced features.

All of these limitations can lead businesses to look for Shopify alternatives that are more flexible, have better pricing structures, or have specific functionality that Shopify doesn’t offer.

Alternatives to Shopify: Complete Comparison

3 Best Alternatives to Shopify in 2024

While there are many Shopify alternatives in the market, these three platforms stand out for their robust features, flexibility, and scalability.

1. Magento

Magento (now Adobe Commerce) is a powerful eCommerce solution that is an open-source platform and provides full control over your store. Due to its strong structure and high degree of flexibility, Magento is suitable for big companies and companies that have many requirements.

How is Magento Different From Shopify? 

  • Flexibility: It provides the ability to fine-tune every single thing in your store and offers a refined experience to the customers. Magento can be easily customized according to the needs of a business.
  • Scalability: Magento is highly scalable and ideal for any business planning to expand shortly. The basic idea in developing Magento was that it can manage an unlimited number of items, no matter if they are few or even in thousands, without creating a lag in the website.
  • No Transaction Fees: While Shopify has its advantages, one of the main disadvantages is that it charges transaction fees, something that does not apply to Magento and can be a great plus for companies with high turnover. It is among the best cheap alternatives to Shopify.

Best For:

  • Large enterprises
  • Businesses requiring extensive customization
  • Companies with dedicated development resources

2. WooCommerce

WooCommerce is an excellent e-commerce plugin for WordPress and, therefore, a very versatile tool for any business that has its website on the WordPress platform. It is open-source and free software that is suitable for small and medium-scale enterprises.

How is Woocommerce Different From Shopify? 

  • Cost-Effective: The WooCommerce platform itself is free, but you might have to spend money on hosting, themes, and plugins. Still, it is cheaper than Shopify, especially for a new business with a small budget.
  • WordPress Integration: If you are using WordPress, then WooCommerce is fully compatible, and you will be able to control content and commerce in one place.
  • Customization: WooCommerce is an open source, so a lot of things can be customized in it. In addition, you will have access to a large collection of plugins and themes that will help you build the store of your dreams.

Best For:

  • Small to medium-sized businesses
  • WordPress users
  • Businesses needing flexible content management and e-commerce in one

3. BigCommerce

BigCommerce is powerful, scalable, and an all-in-one e-commerce solution for businesses of all sizes. It is meant for not only small but also large corporations, combining ease of use with potent features.

How is BigCommerce Different From Shopify? 

  • Built-In Features: One of the BigCommerce advantages is that it does not collect any kind of transaction fees, even if you use a third-party payment gateway. That is to say, BigCommerce would be suitable for high turnover merchants.
  • No Transaction Fees: It does not take any transaction fees, even if you link your account with a third-party payment gateway.
  • Scalability: BigCommerce is scalable and provides you with all the tools and features you may need for your store to grow.

Best For:

  • Small to large enterprises
  • Businesses looking for built-in features
  • Companies planning to scale rapidly

Which Shopify Alternative to Pick?

Choosing the right substitution for Shopify has to be a function of the concrete needs of your business. However, if you are looking for a very flexible, scalable, and yet affordable platform, then Magento certainly fits this description.

This is particularly helpful to big enterprises or companies with unique needs that go beyond what Shopify offers.

It is thus important to choose the one that will be in harmony with your business objectives, the technical infrastructure available, and the resources that you can spare.
